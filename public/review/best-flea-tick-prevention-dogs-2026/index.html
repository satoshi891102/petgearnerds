Do Natural Alternatives Work?

We asked our vet panel about natural alternatives (essential oils, diatomaceous earth, garlic, brewer's yeast). The consensus was clear:

âš ï¸ Vet consensus: Natural alternatives are NOT reliable flea/tick prevention.
Essential oils can be toxic to dogs (especially tea tree, pennyroyal, and clove). Garlic is toxic in quantities needed for pest control. Diatomaceous earth can irritate lungs. None have peer-reviewed evidence of efficacy comparable to pharmaceutical preventatives.

That said, environmental management (regular vacuuming, washing bedding, treating your yard) is a legitimate complement to pharmaceutical prevention.
