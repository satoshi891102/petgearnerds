Bottom line: According to the ASPCA, 1 in 3 pets will get lost during their lifetime. GPS trackers provide real-time location data, escape alerts, and activity monitoring. We tested 5 trackers over 4 weeks with active dogs in urban, suburban, and rural environments.

How GPS Dog Trackers Work

All pet GPS trackers use cellular networks (LTE) to transmit location data to your phone. They don't use your phone's Bluetooth ‚Äî they have their own SIM cards and require monthly subscriptions ($5-13/month). The tracker determines position via GPS satellites, then sends coordinates over the cell network to the companion app.

GPS Tracker vs. Microchip

A microchip is NOT a tracker ‚Äî it's a passive RFID tag that can only be read at close range with a scanner (at a vet clinic or shelter). It helps identify your dog after they're found. A GPS tracker helps you find your dog in the first place. You need both.

‚úÖ What GPS Trackers Can Do

  • Real-time location tracking
  • Escape alerts within seconds
  • Activity monitoring (steps, rest, play)
  • Location history

‚ùå What GPS Trackers Can't Do

  • Work without cellular coverage
  • Replace a microchip (ID + medical records)
  • Track indoor location precisely
  • Work on very small dogs/cats (most are too heavy)
