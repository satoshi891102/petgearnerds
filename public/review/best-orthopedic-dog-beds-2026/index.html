Why Orthopedic Beds Matter

If your dog is over 7 years old, weighs more than 50 pounds, or has joint issues like arthritis or hip dysplasia, a standard dog bed isn't cutting it. Orthopedic beds use memory foam or specialized foam layers that distribute weight evenly and reduce pressure on joints.

The problem? Most "orthopedic" dog beds use cheap foam that compresses within weeks. We've tested beds advertised as orthopedic that were essentially flat pancakes after a month. That's why long-term testing matters.

How to Choose the Right Orthopedic Bed

Consider Your Dog's Weight

This is the most important factor. A 30-pound dog and a 100-pound dog need completely different foam densities. Beds rated for "all sizes" typically compromise â€” too firm for small dogs, too soft for large dogs. Buy size-specific when possible.

Foam Type Matters

Memory foam conforms to your dog's body shape â€” great for arthritis and joint issues. Orthopedic foam (high-density polyurethane) provides firm support without conforming. Egg-crate foam is the budget option with basic pressure distribution. For senior dogs with real joint issues, memory foam or specialized therapeutic foam is worth the investment.

Check the Warranty

A foam bed with no warranty or a 90-day warranty is telling you something: the manufacturer knows it'll flatten. Beds with 2+ year warranties (especially "won't flatten" guarantees) use denser, more durable foam. The warranty length is actually a decent proxy for foam quality.
