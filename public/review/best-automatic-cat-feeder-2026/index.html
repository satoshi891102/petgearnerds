Why Automatic Feeders Matter

Portion control is the primary reason. Cats on free-feed (always-available food) have obesity rates of 60%+. Automatic feeders dispense exact portions at exact times, which is the single most effective weight management tool for cats who overeat.

The secondary benefit: schedule consistency. Cats thrive on routine. An automatic feeder delivers food at the same time every day, whether you're home, at work, or traveling. For multi-cat households, timed feeders can separate mealtimes to prevent food bullying.

What to Consider Before Buying

WiFi vs. Non-Connected

WiFi feeders let you adjust schedules remotely, get meal confirmations, and feed on-demand from your phone. Non-connected feeders are simpler and can't be affected by internet outages. If you travel, WiFi is worth it. If the feeder stays home and your routine is consistent, save the money.

Kibble Only vs. Wet Food

Most automatic feeders are kibble-only. If your cat eats wet food, your options are limited to tray-style feeders (Cat Mate C500) or the SureFeed (which seals wet food). Wet food left in an open tray for hours is a bacterial risk â€” only use feeders designed for it.

Power Backup

If you travel and a power outage means your cat doesn't eat, battery backup is non-negotiable. The PetSafe and Cat Mate both have it. Most budget WiFi feeders don't.
