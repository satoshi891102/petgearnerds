What Type of Crate Does Your Dog Need?

Crate selection depends on three factors: your dog's size and temperament, your primary use case (home training vs travel), and whether your dog is a calm settler or a determined escape artist.

Wire crates (MidWest, Diggs) are the standard choice. Excellent ventilation, visibility, and fold-flat portability. Best for: house training, everyday use, calm to moderate temperament dogs.

Plastic crates (Petmate Sky Kennel) are IATA airline-approved and feel more den-like. Less ventilation but more privacy, which some anxious dogs prefer. Best for: airline travel, dogs who feel calmer in enclosed spaces.

Heavy-duty crates (Impact, ProSelect Empire) are built for dogs that destroy standard crates. Aluminum construction, reinforced latches, escape-proof design. Best for: separation anxiety, power chewers, dogs with history of crate escape.

Soft-sided crates (EliteField) are lightweight travel crates for calm dogs. Not escape-proof â€” a determined dog can claw through the mesh. Best for: camping, road trips, temporary use with relaxed dogs.

Crate Sizing Guide

The most common crate mistake: buying too large. A crate for house training should be big enough for your dog to stand, turn around, and lie down â€” that's it. Too much extra space and your puppy will pee in one corner and sleep in the other, defeating the purpose.
