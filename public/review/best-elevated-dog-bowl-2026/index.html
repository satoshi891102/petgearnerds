The Height Formula

The correct bowl height is your dog's elbow height minus 4-6 inches. This allows a natural, slightly downward head position that aids digestion without straining the neck. Measure from the floor to your dog's elbow joint, subtract 5 inches, and that's your target bowl rim height.

Bloat Warning: What to Know

Gastric dilatation-volvulus (GDV/bloat) is a life-threatening emergency where the stomach twists. Breeds at highest risk: Great Danes, German Shepherds, Standard Poodles, Setters, Weimaraners, and Saint Bernards.

For these breeds, consult your vet before using elevated bowls. The Purdue study found elevated feeding was associated with a 52% increased risk of bloat in large breeds â€” but the study has been critiqued for methodology issues, and many veterinary orthopedists still recommend elevation for dogs with cervical pain or arthritis.

Our recommendation: For at-risk breeds, use only slight elevation (elbow height minus 6 inches, not minus 4). For all others, elevation at the proper height is generally beneficial for comfort.
